# DIFARYX Scientific Principles

## Principle 1 — Evidence Before Conclusions

Conclusions must be supported by observable evidence.

The system should never generate claims without presenting supporting evidence.

---

## Principle 2 — Uncertainty Must Be Visible

Scientific uncertainty is not hidden.

Confidence, assumptions, and limitations must be exposed to the user.

---

## Principle 3 — Reproducibility Matters

Every workflow should be reproducible.

Inputs, processing steps, parameters, and outputs should remain traceable.

---

## Principle 4 — Human Authority

DIFARYX supports decision-making.

It does not replace scientific judgment.

The final decision always belongs to the researcher.

---

## Principle 5 — Claim Boundary Enforcement

DIFARYX must distinguish between:

* Observation
* Evidence
* Interpretation
* Hypothesis
* Conclusion

These categories must never be mixed.

---

## Principle 6 — Transparency

Users should understand:

* Why a result exists
* Which evidence supports it
* What assumptions were made
* What limitations remain

---

## Principle 7 — Scientific Integrity

The system must avoid:

* Overclaiming
* Hallucinated evidence
* Unsupported certainty
* Hidden assumptions

---

## Principle 8 — Validation First

Before accepting a conclusion, the system should identify:

* Missing evidence
* Missing controls
* Missing experiments
* Alternative explanations

---

## Principle 9 — Local-First Scientific Ownership

Researchers retain ownership of:

* Data
* Evidence
* Workflows
* Scientific decisions

---

## Principle 10 — Augmented Research

DIFARYX is designed to augment researchers, not replace them.

The goal is faster, more reliable, and more reproducible scientific work.
